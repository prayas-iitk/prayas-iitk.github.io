Learn About Prayas

PRAYAS is an initiative aimed at providing education to the underprivileged and marginalized children living in and around the campus. Started in 2001 by a few passionate students, the organization conducts regular evening classes for around 120 children from classes 1 to 12 where the emphasis is to teach through various fun-filled activities.

History

The Vivekananda Committee has been working to connect children deprived of education at IIT Kanpur from 1990-1991 onwards. During 1990-1992, with the cooperation of students of IITK, started the task of providing quality education to the underprivileged children and this task of connecting children to education emerged as an "effort". In "Prayas", children from Nanakari, Barasirohi, Lodhar, kilns and surrounding areas come to study. Besides education, “Prayas” conducts many other activities to promote a holistic development in kids and consequently, students who have graduated from “Prayas” are holding prestigious positions in diverse fields and are educating other kids as well. The activity of teaching at “Prayas” has been going on for almost 20 years and this work will continue incessantly so that children deprived of education can be taken from darkness to light, only then we will be able to improve ourselves and society.
Salute to Prayas team for igniting many minds to reach their potentials.
--- Vijaya Didi

Introduction

PRAYAS is an endeavor of IIT Kanpur (IITK) students, focused on providing education to the kids from marginalized sections of society in and around IITK campus. Started in 2001 and presently a club under the Presidential Council in the Students' Gymkhana, IIT Kanpur, Prayas is a voluntary service to promote the notion of 'Education for All' as a sustainable means of development. The motto of PRAYAS is 'to Inspire and Enable'

How can you help?

VOLUNTEER

There are host of activities in which you can volunteer:

1. Teach ANY subject of your choice to ANY class at the evening school. Everything is managed by the student volunteers only so everything is completely flexible.

2. Moral Education: Narrate a story and give the kids lessons of life. More important than any other class, these 15-20 minute sessions before every evening class are the fastest and sweetest way to volunteer.

3. Anything else you want to involve in. Talk to the other volunteers and share with them any constructive idea you have.

DONATE

Its not only money which you can give.....

1. Old story books and magazines which you think can be of value to kids

2. Children love drawing and we are always short of blank pages. Pages from your examination copies, even slightest thing like back side of notices which are put up everywhere in your halls are very useful. Do spare a moment to collect such things and contact us.

3. Purchase beautiful greetings and friendship bands made from drawings of the children.
